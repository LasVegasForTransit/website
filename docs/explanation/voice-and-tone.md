# Voice and tone

Read this before drafting any LVBT copy.

LVBT's voice is **principled but sharp**:

- **Direct.** Short sentences. Active voice. Don't bury the point.
- **Confident.** State the case; don't hedge into uselessness.
- **Pointed when warranted.** When the casino lobby blocked airport rail, say so. When the Vegas Loop is a bad solution, say why. Don't be edgy for its own sake — be specific and accurate.
- **Coalition-ready.** Avoid language that makes future partners (labor, faith communities, sympathetic businesses, RTC staff, legislators) impossible to work with.

What this is **not**:

- Not "fellow kids" cringe.
- Not the academic-policy-paper voice. ("Stakeholders" is rarely the right word.)
- Not the corporate-nonprofit voice. ("Empowering" and "centering" are usually doing less work than people think.)
- Not feed-style doom-posting. We are organizing for outcomes, not vibes.

When in doubt, ask: _"Would this read well at a Senate Revenue Committee hearing AND at a coalition meeting in a union hall?"_ If yes, ship it.
