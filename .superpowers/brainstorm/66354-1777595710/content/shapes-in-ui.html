P1 · Diamond divider

Replaces or extends the 6px ink rule

Five small ink diamonds with a gold one in the middle. More ornament than the existing rule; saves the rule for "load-bearing" hierarchy moments.

P2 · Marquee CTA button

Bulb-bordered primary action

For the one or two highest-priority CTAs per page (Join, Donate, Sign petition). Other actions stay as the existing flat outline buttons.

P3 · Capsule letters

For category and step labels

Used for top-level section labels (NEWS, JOIN, ACT) and step numbers (01, 02, 03). The most direct lift from the WELCOME treatment.

  • Real bus rapid transit on Maryland Parkway
  • 15-minute frequency on every primary corridor
  • Light rail Strip ↔ downtown ↔ airport
P4 · Star bullets

For principles and demands lists

Reserve for "manifesto" content (vision, demands, principles) — not generic bullet lists. Keeps the device meaningful instead of decorative.

P5 · Diamond stat block

For headline numbers

Big single statistics get the diamond-and-bulbs treatment. Replaces or augments the existing huge clamp-display StatBlock.

P6 · Diamond image frame

For hero photos and portraits

Photos cropped to diamond with bulb border. For people (board portraits, testimonials), places (Maryland Parkway, mountains), and key moments.

P7 · Bulb dash

For underlines, progress, route lines

A row of small bulbs as an alternative to the 1px gray rule or the 2px link underline. Especially good for route diagrams (each bulb = a stop) and progress indicators.

If this direction resonates, the next move is consolidating into a single design — a palette/tone choice from sections A–F + the shape primitives here. Tell me your read and we'll lock the design.
