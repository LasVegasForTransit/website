F1 · Reverent homage

Sign stays sacred. We sit beside it.

The actual Welcome sign — text untouched — paired with the LVBT wordmark as a sibling. The brand says: we are of this place, we honor what's here, we're not subverting it. Lowest political risk. The sign reads as an authority co-sign rather than a punchline.

F2 · Activated subversion

The brand lives inside the sign.

One letter changed in the centerpiece (VEGAS → VEGANS), "Fabulous" swapped for "BETTER" — captures the entire LVBT thesis in 7 words. Most distinctive, highest meme velocity, also highest risk of reading as parody. Probably best used selectively (homepage hero, t-shirts, anniversary moments) rather than as the everyday mark.

F4 · Brand system in action

How the pieces compose.

The sign isn't a logo — it's a system. Wordmark in the header (everyday). Diamond silhouette as favicon/byline mark. Full activated sign as a hero element on the homepage. Capsule letters as a typographic device for labels (NEWS, JOIN, ACTS). Bulb-bordered frames for callouts and CTAs. Each piece works alone; together they're unmistakably one brand.
